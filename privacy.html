WitnessRecord Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

WitnessRecord ("the app") is built to help people document encounters with law enforcement and preserve that footage off-device. This policy explains what data the app handles, where it goes, and who can see it.

Summary

  • WitnessRecord does not have a server. There is no backend operated by the developer.
  • Video, audio, location, and event logs the app records go directly from your device to your own Google Drive account — nowhere else.
  • The developer of WitnessRecord cannot see, access, or receive your recordings, your location, or your Google account data at any point.
  • The app requests the Google Drive drive.file scope only, which limits it to files the app itself creates. It cannot read, list, or modify any other file in your Drive.

The app does not collect analytics, does not use advertising or tracking SDKs, and does not transmit any data to the developer or any third party other than Google (for the sole purpose of uploading files to your own Drive account).

Google Drive access

WitnessRecord uses Google Sign-In to request the drive.file OAuth scope. This is Google's most restricted Drive scope: it only grants access to files and folders the app itself creates in your Drive. The app cannot see your existing files, folders, or any other data in your Google account.

You can revoke this access at any time from your Google Account's Third-Party Apps & Services settings, or by deleting the WitnessRecord folder from your Drive. Revoking access does not delete footage already uploaded to your Drive — that footage is yours, stored in your own account, and is managed the same as any other file in your Drive.

Local storage

Video is recorded in short chunks on your device and deleted automatically once Google Drive confirms it has received both the video and its accompanying metadata file. If the device has no network connection, chunks remain in local storage until a connection is available, so no footage is deleted before it is safely uploaded.

Photos library

Unless you turn it off in the app's settings, WitnessRecord also saves a full copy of each recording session to your device's Photos library, as an on-device backup independent of Google Drive. The app requests only "add-only" Photos access: it can add its own recordings to your library, but it cannot see, read, or access any of your existing photos or videos.

If you have iCloud Photos enabled on your device, Apple will sync these recordings to your iCloud account like any other video in your library, subject to Apple's Privacy Policy. You can turn off the Photos backup in the app's settings while still uploading to Google Drive, and you can delete saved recordings from your Photos library at any time.

Location data

WitnessRecord requests "Always" location access so it can continue logging location while the screen is off (recording runs with the display dimmed) or the app is briefly backgrounded. Location is used exclusively to timestamp and geotag recordings for evidentiary purposes. It is not used for any other purpose, is not shared with any third party, and is not used to track you outside of active recording sessions.

Data retention and deletion

Because recordings are stored in your own Google Drive, you control retention and deletion directly through Drive — the developer has no copy of your data to retain or delete. Deleting a file from your Drive removes it from Google's storage subject to Google's own retention policies (see Google's Privacy Policy, linked below).

Children's privacy

WitnessRecord is not directed at children and is not intended for use by anyone under 13.
