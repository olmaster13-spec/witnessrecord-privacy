WitnessRecord

Document encounters. Preserve the footage — even if your phone doesn't make it.

WitnessRecord is an iOS app for civilians documenting police encounters. It records video with the screen fully black, and uploads the footage to your own Google Drive in small pieces while you're still recording — so what you've captured is already safe off-device if your phone is seized, destroyed, or lost.

How it works

Instant start

Bind recording to the iPhone Action Button, a lock screen widget, or "Hey Siri, start recording with WitnessRecord." No unlocking, no menus — the app launches straight into recording.

Black screen while recording

The display goes dark at minimum brightness for the entire recording. To anyone watching, the phone looks idle. Triple-tap and hold to stop — hard to trigger by accident.

Footage leaves the device as it's captured

Video is recorded in ~25-second chunks that upload immediately over cellular or Wi-Fi. Nothing waits for the recording to end. If there's no signal, chunks queue locally and upload the moment connectivity returns — nothing is deleted until your Drive confirms it arrived.

Built for evidence

Every chunk is fingerprinted with a SHA-256 hash and paired with a record of when and where it was captured — a basic chain-of-custody trail, with GPS and UTC timestamps embedded in the video metadata. The frame itself stays clean, with no visible overlay.

Your data stays yours

WitnessRecord has no server. Footage goes directly from your device to your own Google Drive account and nowhere else. The developer cannot see or access your recordings, your location, or your Google account.

The app requests Google's most restricted Drive permission (drive.file), which only allows access to files the app itself creates.

Full details: Privacy Policy

Know your rights

In most U.S. states, openly recording on-duty police officers in public is constitutionally protected activity, so long as you don't physically interfere. Laws vary by state and change over time — see the ACLU's know-your-rights guidance for current information.

WitnessRecord provides no legal advice. Stay at a safe distance, and announce that you are recording if it is safe to do so.
